# Coverage Calculator - User Guide

## Getting Started

The Coverage Calculator helps you estimate how much your insurance will cover for a medical treatment and what you'll pay out-of-pocket.

### Accessing the Calculator

1. **From Sidebar**: Click "Coverage Calculator" in the left menu
2. **Direct URL**: Visit `/coverage-calculator`
3. **Mobile**: Tap the menu icon (☰) then select Coverage Calculator

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Enter Your Diagnosis

**Field**: Medical Diagnosis  
**What to enter**: The medical condition or treatment you need

**Examples**:
- "Appendectomy" (removal of appendix)
- "Knee Replacement"
- "Type 2 Diabetes Management"
- "Cataract Surgery"

---

### Step 2: Select Treatment Type

**Field**: Treatment Type  
**Importance**: This determines how much insurance typically covers for this type of care

**Available Options**:
- **Surgery** - 100% base coverage (very common)
- **Hospitalization** - 100% base coverage
- **Emergency Care** - 100% base coverage
- **Inpatient Care** - 90% base coverage
- **Outpatient Care** - 80% base coverage
- **Diagnostic Tests** - 90% base coverage
- **Prescription Drugs** - 75% base coverage
- **Rehabilitation** - 80% base coverage
- **Mental Health** - 70% base coverage
- **Preventive Care** - 60% base coverage

**Pro Tip**: Not sure? Check the "Quick Tips" section on the right for explanations.

---

### Step 3: Enter Estimated Cost

**Field**: Estimated Cost  
**What to enter**: The total cost of the medical treatment (in dollars)

**How to find this**:
- Ask your hospital or clinic for an estimate
- Check your insurance provider's cost estimator tool
- Use healthcare cost databases (e.g., Healthcare Bluebook)

**Example**: "$15,000" for surgery

---

### Step 4: Select Your Policy Type

**Field**: Policy Type  
**Importance**: Determines the base coverage percentage

**Available Options**:

#### Comprehensive Coverage
- **Best for**: People with frequent medical needs
- **Insurance covers**: 85% of eligible costs
- **Good if**: You want maximum coverage
- **Exclusions**: Cosmetic, experimental treatments

#### Standard Coverage ⭐ (Most Common)
- **Best for**: Average medical needs
- **Insurance covers**: 70% of eligible costs
- **Good if**: You want balanced coverage
- **Exclusions**: More than comprehensive

#### Basic Coverage
- **Best for**: Young, healthy people
- **Insurance covers**: 60% of eligible costs
- **Good if**: You want lower premiums
- **Exclusions**: Includes preventive care limitations

#### Catastrophic Coverage
- **Best for**: Emergency-only protection
- **Insurance covers**: 50% of eligible costs
- **Good if**: You're young and want very low cost
- **Exclusions**: Many types of care limited

---

### Step 5: Set Your Policy Details

These are typically found on your insurance card or policy document.

#### Annual Deductible
**What it is**: Money you must pay before insurance starts covering

**Typical amounts**:
- Low: $500
- Medium: $1,000
- High: $2,500+

**Example**: If your deductible is $1,000 and you need a $5,000 treatment:
- You pay: $1,000
- Insurance pays: $4,000 (after that)

#### Policy Max Limit
**What it is**: Maximum amount insurance will pay per year

**Typical amounts**:
- $50,000
- $100,000
- $250,000
- Unlimited (varies by policy)

**Example**: If your max is $100,000 and treatment costs $150,000:
- Insurance pays: $100,000
- You pay: $50,000

#### Coinsurance
**What it is**: Your share of costs after meeting deductible (as percentage)

**Typical amounts**:
- 10% (you pay 10%, insurance pays 90%)
- 20% (you pay 20%, insurance pays 80%)
- 30% (you pay 30%, insurance pays 70%)

**Example**: With 20% coinsurance on a $10,000 treatment:
- You pay: $2,000
- Insurance pays: $8,000

---

### Step 6: Click Calculate Coverage

Click the blue "Calculate Coverage" button to see your results.

The calculator will analyze your information and show:
- How much insurance covers
- How much you pay
- Visual breakdown of costs
- Special conditions or warnings
- Recommendations for you

---

## Understanding Your Results

### The Summary Cards

You'll see 4 cards at the top showing key numbers:

#### Insurance Covers
- Dollar amount insurance will pay
- Percentage of total treatment cost
- This is what you want to be high

#### You Pay (Deductible)
- Amount you must pay first
- Before insurance helps
- Resets every year

#### You Pay (Coinsurance)
- Your percentage share after deductible
- Goes toward your out-of-pocket max
- Varies by policy

#### Total Patient Cost
- Everything you must pay out-of-pocket
- Deductible + coinsurance
- Plan your finances accordingly

---

### The Charts

#### Cost Breakdown (Bar Chart)
Shows where every dollar of your estimated cost goes:
- **Insurance Covers**: Green (good)
- **Deductible**: Orange (you pay first)
- **Coinsurance**: Red (your percentage share)
- **Uncovered**: Gray (not covered at all)

#### Coverage Split (Pie Chart)
Simple visual showing:
- **Insurance Coverage**: Green portion
- **Patient Responsibility**: Red portion

**Good news**: If insurance covers more than 70%, it's excellent coverage

---

### Important Warnings

#### ⚠️ Policy Limit Exceeded
Your treatment costs more than your yearly maximum.
**What to do**:
- Discuss costs with your hospital
- Ask about payment plans
- Consider alternatives (if applicable)

#### ❌ Coverage Exclusions
Your policy doesn't cover certain aspects.
**What to do**:
- Ask your insurance what IS covered
- Look for approved alternatives
- Check if pre-authorization is needed

---

### Recommendations

The calculator provides personalized suggestions:

**If coverage is low**:
- Consider upgrading your plan next year
- Save for medical expenses

**If deductible is high**:
- Set aside emergency medical savings
- Check if a Health Savings Account (HSA) helps

**If coinsurance is high**:
- Ask if your policy has better cost-sharing options
- Look for negotiated rates at preferred providers

**For expensive treatments**:
- Check if multiple payment plans are available
- Ask about financial assistance programs

## Common Questions

### Q: Why does one treatment cost differ from another?

**A**: Different treatment types have different "usual and customary" costs. Surgery is typically well-covered (100% base), while dental or vision might be 50% or less.

---

### Q: What if my actual bill is different from the estimate?

**A**: This calculator estimates based on typical costs. Your actual bill may vary due to:
- Complications requiring extra care
- Different provider charges
- Regional price differences

Contact your provider for a formal estimate before treatment.

---

### Q: Does this include network/non-network differences?

**A**: Not currently. This calculator assumes in-network care. Out-of-network costs are typically much higher. **Always verify your provider is in-network before treatment.**

---

### Q: Can I compare different insurance plans?

**A**: Yes! Run the calculator with each plan's details to compare. You can:
1. Calculate first plan
2. Take note of the insurance coverage amount
3. Reset and calculate with second plan
4. Compare the results

---

### Q: What does "out-of-pocket maximum" mean?

**A**: The most you'll pay in a year (deductible + coinsurance). After you hit this, insurance pays 100%. This isn't shown directly but affects your total financial responsibility.

---

### Q: Does pre-existing conditions affect coverage?

**A**: This calculator doesn't adjust for pre-existing conditions (yet). In the US, they can't be excluded, but coverage might differ. **Check your specific policy document.**

---

### Q: Can I download the results?

**A**: Yes! Click "Download Report" and you'll get a text file with:
- Your claim details
- Coverage breakdown
- Full calculations
- Date generated

## Tips for Accurate Estimates

### 1. Get an Actual Quote
Don't guess the cost. Ask your:
- Hospital billing department
- Doctor's office
- Insurance company's cost estimator tool

### 2. Verify Your Policy Numbers
Check your insurance card for:
- Deductible
- Out-of-pocket maximum
- Coinsurance percentage

### 3. Confirm Coverage Type
Different plans have different rules:
- Major medical vs. limited benefit
- In-network vs. out-of-network
- Specific exclusions for your condition

### 4. Check for Pre-Authorization
Some treatments need approval first:
- Call your insurance before scheduling
- Ask if treatment requires authorization
- Get written approval

### 5. Ask About Financial Assistance
Many providers offer:
- Payment plans (no interest)
- Charity care programs
- Negotiated rates for uninsured

## Troubleshooting

### Calculator won't load?
1. Refresh the page
2. Check you're using a supported browser
3. Clear browser cache

### Results seem wrong?
1. Verify you entered correct policy details
2. Check your insurance card for accurate numbers
3. Contact your insurance company to confirm

### Chart won't display?
1. Enable JavaScript in browser settings
2. Try a different browser
3. Check your internet connection

### Download button doesn't work?
1. Check if pop-ups are blocked
2. Try a different browser
3. Right-click button and "Save link as"

## Important Disclaimers

This calculator provides **estimates only** and:
- May not reflect your exact policy terms
- Doesn't account for pre-existing conditions
- Doesn't include network/non-network differences
- Doesn't consider prior authorization requirements
- Doesn't account for regional price variations

**Always**:
- Contact your insurance company for exact costs
- Get written approval before treatment
- Ask for itemized bills after treatment
- Review your Explanation of Benefits (EOB)
